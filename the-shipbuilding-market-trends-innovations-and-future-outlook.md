<h2>Market Overview</h2>
<p>Shipbuilding is a vital industry involving the design, construction, and maintenance of various types of ships, including&nbsp;<strong>commercial vessels, military ships, and offshore structures</strong>. The market is driven by factors such as rising seaborne trade, increasing maritime security concerns, and advancements in&nbsp;<strong>green shipping technologies</strong>.</p>
<h2>Key Market Trends</h2>
<h3>1.&nbsp;<strong>Rise in Demand for Green Ships</strong></h3>
<p>Environmental regulations and sustainability concerns are pushing the industry toward&nbsp;<strong>eco-friendly ships</strong>&nbsp;powered by&nbsp;<strong>LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas), hydrogen fuel cells, and electric propulsion systems</strong>.</p>
<h3>2.&nbsp;<strong>Integration of Smart Ship Technologies</strong></h3>
<p>The adoption of&nbsp;<strong>automation, IoT, AI-driven navigation, and real-time monitoring systems</strong>&nbsp;is enhancing ship efficiency, reducing operational costs, and improving safety.</p>
<h3>3.&nbsp;<strong>Growing Investments in Naval Defense</strong></h3>
<p>With increasing geopolitical tensions, governments are investing heavily in&nbsp;<strong>warships, submarines, and advanced naval vessels</strong>, driving growth in the military segment of the shipbuilding market.</p>
<h3>4.&nbsp;<strong>Expansion of Offshore Energy Sector</strong></h3>
<p>The rising demand for&nbsp;<strong>offshore wind farms and oil &amp; gas exploration</strong>&nbsp;is increasing the need for specialized vessels such as&nbsp;<strong>offshore support ships and floating production units</strong>.</p>
