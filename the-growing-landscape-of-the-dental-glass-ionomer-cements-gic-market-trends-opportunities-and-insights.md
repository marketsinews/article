<h3><strong>What Are Dental Glass Ionomer Cements (GIC)?</strong></h3>
<p>Dental Glass Ionomer Cements are versatile materials widely used in dentistry for restorative procedures, luting, and preventive applications. Composed of a mixture of glass powder and an organic acid, GICs offer unique properties such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Adhesion to tooth structure</strong>: GICs chemically bond to both enamel and dentin, reducing the need for extensive tooth preparation.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Fluoride release</strong>: They release fluoride over time, which helps prevent secondary caries and strengthens tooth structure.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Biocompatibility</strong>: GICs are well-tolerated by oral tissues, making them suitable for a wide range of patients.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Ease of use</strong>: Their simple application process makes them a popular choice among dental professionals.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>These properties make GICs an ideal choice for various dental applications, including fillings, cementation of crowns and bridges, and fissure sealants.</p>
